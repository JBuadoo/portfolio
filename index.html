Tap In: A music promotion
and discovery platform

Music is growing in unique ways, and that growth has no where to flourish. "Tap In" is a music promotion & discussion platform that can help artists grown their platforms whiles helping listeners find new music and artists. As the visonary behind the platform, I wanted to blend older design styles with a modern flare. This was a class project for Interaction Design II, where I led a group of 3 other individuals to create a working prototype, using the Lean UX process. This project spanned from January 2026 to April 2026, using Figjam and Figma for research and building.

Interaction Design II • Jan 2026 - Apr 2026
• Showcase

The Problem:

Like mentioned earlier, the music industry needs a place to grow, and it lacks a dedicated space to do so.

  • Growth is difficult in the music industry, especially now in the digital age.
  • Everyone finds new music in unique ways, but it's not a consistent process.
  • Artists and fans go elsewhere to build communities in spaces that wasn't specifically made for them.
  • It's hard for artists and those who want to start to get the connections they need to create more work.

Solutions:

Addressing the core needs of artists and listeners.

🌟

Spotlight

Created a way for artists to promote their music with fair chance at exposure, whiles listeners can find new artists, songs and genres, being mutually beneficial for both parties.

🔀

Randomization

Discoverability goes further with randomized suggestions, making the process enjoyable, whiles still promoting artists and their music.

🤝

Connecting

Helping creatives connect with each other for collabs, work, or help with communication features.

💬

Discussions

Housing music enthusiast in a place dedicated for them to them to discuss with others about music, with also having integrated tools just for them.

🌍

Community

Giving users a chance to build community with others in a space that is meant for them, especially with the removable of Twitter communities, which was where many music enthusiast use for this need.

Lessons Learned:

  • As team leader, I learned a lot with what comes with management. Delegation of tasks is important, and it's also important to build upon teammates strengths.
  • With the timeframe of this project, scaling down on the prototype had to be done, but it's not the end of the world. This was a disappointing aspect for me at first, but I'm still proud of everything that got done.
  • With interviews and usability tests, every participant is different. These interviews and tests taught me how to fish for information we need, without explicitly asking. I've conducted many before this project, but this was the most I've ever had for a project. By the last few, I realized my process wasn't the best, and was giving us feedback that varied on how helpful it was. By the end, I readjusted, and we got the best information back we got the whole semester.
  • I wanted to go beyond my previous projects and add a lot more animations. I'm the type to learn by just messing around on a software, and with reptation. I still need to work on file hygiene, but I've developed a lot more skills in terms of creating unique interactions and animations.
  • It's okay to take a pause or a break. I do music production and it's commonly practiced to get up and revisit later. The end product is better, and this can be applied to creating work in general. I had a habit of sitting on the file until I finish what I sought out to do for the day. This caused fatigue, and I had to learn to step away.

Mixr: A new way to discover music

Finding new music can be a drag in this oversaturated environment. "Mixr" tackles that drag and helps users discover new music, with a platform built for discussion. This was a group project for our Senior Capstone Showcase to create a working prototype, following the Goal-Directed Design method to conduct research, user interviews, and usability tests. This project spanned from January 2026 to April 2026, using Figjam, Google Forms, Google Docs, and Figma for research and building.

Capstone Showcase • January 2026 - April 2026

The Problem:

Certain suggestion methods aren't cutting it, and it's difficult to find something that speaks to you.

  • On main stream platforms, suggestions are hit or miss, usually dishing users what is popular, instead of what is tailored to the listener.
  • Discussion is often limited, if it exists at all on a music platform.
  • Many platforms usually have features locked behind a paywall, limiting the typical user immensely.
  • Many platforms expect users to understand how to use them, often having poor learnability.

Solutions:

How we approached these issues.

🧠

Tailored feeds

Feed is based off the users interests from what they selected when making an account, and from their interactions on the platform.

💬

Expanded Discussion

Fan pages dedicated to discussions based on music, beyond the platforms review capabilities.

🔓

Open Platform

Nothing was made on this platform with the intentions of locking it behind a paywall. Everyone can enjoy and take part in all features.

✨

Simplistic UI

Simple interface for learnability, with items clearly labeled to ease new users onto the platform.

Lessons Learned:

  • Spring 2026 was easily the most mentally taxing semester of my life (and I did architecture, so that's saying something). Whiles being apart of the Mixr group, I was leading a group for another prototype called Tap In, I was building my portfolio, doing various other projects in Visual Design II, then my other classes. I had to learn how to balance projects, or else I would of sank. At first, I was prioritizing other projects over another, but I found a way to balance the work, and give all my projects the attention that they needed.
  • Every team is different, and I've heard that it sucks having a really good team because you'll always compare your future teams to them. With all the teams I've been apart of, I had to learn how to adjust to a new environment and dynamic. Just like snowflakes, every team is different and unique in it's own regard.
  • I like to know what I'm doing and what is needed from me. This class however didn't give me such luxury. This pushed me to be proactive and be more on top of things since we weren't give a clear process to follow. The team knew the end goal, and we had to work together to find a way to make it there.
  • Flexibility is not a foreign skills of mine, but this project was a nice reminder of that. Things change constantly in life, and you need to be able to readjust to keep the train moving.

Reel: A Movie and Show discussion
rival for Letterboxd

Letterboxd is the go to movie review platform, and we think that should change. Reel is a team made mobile prototype that pushed us to create a movie and TV show reviewing prototype to challenge Letterboxd. We followed the Goal-Directed Design method to conduct research, user interviews, and usability testing to bring this platform to life. This project spanned from August 2025 to November 2025, using Figjam and Figma for research and building.

Interaction Design I • Aug 2025 - Nov 2025
•

The Problem:

Our focus derived from fixing and building upon the issues our biggest competitors have.

  • These platforms often target movies alone, leaving the world of shows neglected.
  • Letterboxd isn't new user friendly, and has a dated UI/aesthetic.
  • As a discussion platform, Letterboxd is limited in social engagement.

Solutions:

Improving the reviewing and social experience.

📺

Inclusion

A general feed with movies and shows, going beyond that with specifics like anime, which is something Letterboxd neglects.

✨

Clean UI

Simplified for learnability sake with straight to the point information and extra useful content.

💬

Discussions

More discussion features that match universal social media structures.

🔍

Discoverability

Beyond movie suggestions, Reel shows upcoming content with where they'll release. This is the type of information that users go elsewhere to find.

Lessons Learned:

  • For this project, I was just a team member. I'm used to being the leader or having to take charge, so this team taught me to be a team player which I enjoyed. Communication is key. Bouncing off ideas, helping others, and learning to accept that not everyone will see your vision.
  • Variables should be set up before getting deep into the project! I wanted to add a theme change, but this was a late addition. Adding variables to components and frames towards the end was a pain. This also resulted in me missing a few spots.
  • Taking notes for tasks is essential. I love making lists, but this project taught me how important it is to make sure everything it jotted down. My schedule was packed, and as a group, we forgot some key features we wanted until the very end, which resulted in a rush to implement them.
  • As my childhood idol Sensei Wu would say, "Never put off until tomorrow what can be done today". Naming frames, keeping the file clean, and more house keeping things is important. I'd often do those things later since I enjoy designing and building. To my horror, tiny things stack up, and it gets more stressful to clean up with a deadline steadily approaching.

Milestone Comics Rebrand

Milestone Media was made 1993 by black creatives to fight the issue of exclusion of black characters in comic book media. Static, Icon, Rocket, and Hardware are among the vast cast created by Milestone Media. The company later merged with the DC Universe in 2008. After 18 years later, these legend are back again in the DU Universe, and the brand needs a major revamp. For this project, I was tasked with creating 3 deliverables which were rebranding their website, creating a new character/series, and creating a variant cover for the "New History of the DC Universe: The Dakota Incident" story. I used Figma, photoshop, and Paint.net to create these 3 deliverables.

Visual Design II • March 2026 - April 2026

Milestone Media Website Rebrand

For a comic book companies website, it felt very newspaper press themed. Keeping the the white, black, and red color scheme, with the comic panel collage background, I made a design theg evokes a Rebirth feel. The characters look like they're jumping out of the pages in the background whiles the whole site reads like a fun comic book.

Old Design
New Design

Soul: A New Milestone Series

Milestone has a lot of black culture in it, but as someone from Ghana, I felt like they were missing foreign black culture in their roster. That what inspired SOUL.

SOUL is about a young African American teen name Benjamin Boateng (Benji for short) who's family hails from West Africa. After an intense moment of emotional distress, he awaken laten abilities, granting him to see and move beyond his human limitations. With these new abilities, the world has become much bigger to him, but more dangerous as well. He soon realizes that the ones he hold close to him aren't all pure souls like he thought.

  • When I hear the word soul, I think of ones true self and expression, so the design is Benji's silhouette with extrusion showing who he really is inside.
  • After redesigning the Milestone site, the color red was still hovering in my mind, so I included it to keep brand synergy.
  • The major selling point of SOUL is it's foreign cultural aspects which Milestone is missing. I tried to give the sense of African culture with the line art within Benji's silhouette, which is his soul.

"New History of the DC Universe: The Dakota Incident" Variant Cover

During Black History Month, DC tends to remember their elite cast of black characters and finally give them some story (The last Black led ongoing series at DC ended in 2023, and they haven't made a new one since). During February 2026, I started seeing promotion for this crossover/merger of Milestone and DC, therefore, I created a variant cover as an ad and promotion for the event.
